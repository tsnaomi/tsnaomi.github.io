Hi, my name is Naomi and I am a Seattle-based computational linguist. I recently graduated with an M.S. in Symbolic Systems from Stanford University. Prior to Stanford, I received my B.A. in Linguistics from the University of Washington, then spent a little time in the Seattle tech scene.

My research interests lie at the intersection of natural and artificial linguistic intelligence. I am passionate about projects that creatively combine these areas—namely, language technology that is informed by linguistic insights, and NLP/machine learning systems that shed light on language. I also enjoy making web applications that facilitate work in these areas.

Within formal linguistics, I am drawn to syntax and phonology. When looking at any analysis of language-specific phenomena, I am most curious about its theoretical assumptions and how the analysis bears on the frameworks at play in the field.
