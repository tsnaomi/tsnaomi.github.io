I am a postdoc at the Centre for Language Studies at Radboud Universiteit working with Stefan Frank. My research uses computational and behavioral methods to study how humans and machines learn, represent, and process language.

I recently received my PhD in Computational Linguistics at the University of Washington, where I was advised by Shane Steinert-Threlkeld in his research group, the Computation, Language, & Meaning Band of Researchers (CLMBR). I also worked with Naja Ferjan Ramírez in the Language Development & Processing Lab, founded by the late Akira Omaki, and collaborate frequently with Arto Anttila on the study of syntactic prosody.

Before my PhD, I earned a master's in Symbolic Systems at Stanford University and a bachelor's in Linguistics and Communication at the University of Washington.
