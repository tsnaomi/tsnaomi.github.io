Hi, I'm /naʊmi/. I am a second-year M.S. student in the Symbolic Systems Program at Stanford University. Prior to coming to Stanford, I received my B.A. in Linguistics from the University of Washington, then spent a little time in the Seattle tech scene.

My research interests lie at the intersection of natural and artificial linguistic intelligence. I am enthralled by projects that embody the convergence of these areas — e.g., NLP systems that shed light on formal linguistic theory, and vice versa. For instance, I have been collaborating with Professor Arto Anttila on a Python package that syllabifies Finnish and segments Finnish compounds. A state-of-the-art syllabifier, replete with compound detection, will allow us to probe Finnish phonology on an unprecedented scale. On the flip side, compound segmentation has utility in machine translation, semantic parsing, and spell-checking.

Within formal linguistics, I am drawn to theory-internal questions of phonology and syntax. When looking at an analysis of a language-specific phenomenon, I am most curious about its theoretical assumptions and how the analysis bears on the frameworks at play in the field.
