I am a fourth-year PhD student in Computational Linguistics at the University of Washington.

My research uses computational and experimental methods to study how humans and artificial systems learn, represent, and process language. I work with Shane Steinert-Threlkeld in the Computation, Language, & Meaning Band of Researchers and Naja Ferjan Ramírez in the Language Development & Processing Lab, founded by the late Akira Omaki. I also collaborate frequently with Arto Anttila, applying computational methods to the study of syntactic prosody.

Before pursuing a PhD, I earned a master's in Symbolic Systems at Stanford University and a bachelor's in Linguistics and Communication at the University of Washington.
